Computing Impact on ALFA-Mix

As this project was carried out independently and lacked sufficient computational power to support the execution of Alpha-Mix models effectively, I conducted an experiment. This experiment aimed to assess the impact of enhanced computational resources. Additionally, since the results of Alpha-Mix did not exhibit a significant advantage over random sampling, I designed an experiment involving the CIFAR100 dataset.

In this experiment, I evaluated Vision Transformers under two distinct conditions: one with a 100 epochs, and another with 1000 epochs. The objective was to determine whether Alpha Mix could demonstrate substantial improvements when operating with more robust computational resources.

  • Dataset = CIFAR100
  • Model = Vision Transformers
  • Initial label data = 100 samples
  • Number of queries each round = 1000 samples
  • Number of rounds = 10 rounds
  • Learning rate = 0.001
  • Number of epochs = 100 compare to 1000
  • Strategy = AlphaMixSampling


It can be concluded that the computational power will not affect the result in a significant way.
